PRE-CLASSIC

May 10, 2009 – May 17, 2009

The very first development phase and prototype of Minecraft.
In this phase, the game was still referred to simply as “Cave Game”, later renamed to “Minecraft: Order of the Stone”, and simplified to “Minecraft” at the end.
The game was not available to the public during this phase, the only person able to access it back then being solely Notch, the developer. He uploaded videos of this phase’s development to YouTube in the form of test videos.
This development phase set some of the basic foundations for later Minecraft versions (some were later removed):

  • Physics
  • Blocks: cobblestone, grass, dirt, wooden planks, stone, saplings
  • Mobs: Human
  • Level Generator
  • Particles
  • Human Spawning: pressing (G)
  • The Void
  • The ability to respawn somewhere else on the world by pressing (R)
  • Placing blocks by left-clicking
  • Destroying blocks by right-clicking
In this version it was not yet possible to obtain blocks after breaking them.


CLASSIC

May 17, 2009 – December 23, 2009

This is the second developmental phase of Minecraft, and the first one to be playable by the public on the Minecraft website.
This phase introduced the two basic modes of playing, Survival and Creative. However, Survival mode in this phase had a point-based system.
Classic implemented lots of new changes, but still did not have many of the now defining traits of the game, such as crafting. Instead of a full inventory, the player had a hotbar of 9 preset blocks which could be changed only by cheating.
There were still lots of new additions, though:

  • Blocks: bedrock, water, lava, sand, gravel, ores, trees, sponge, glass, gold block, mushrooms, flowers, signs, mossy cobblestone, TNT, bricks, block of iron, obsidian, bookshelves, stone slabs
  • Mobs: zombies, skeletons, pigs, creepers, spiders, sheep, Rana, giants, beast boy, Steve, Black Steve
  • Items: all weapons
  • New terrain
  • Multiplayer
  • Health bars, damage, dying
  • Removed human spawning
  • Gathering materials in survival mode
  • New textures
In this version the world map was not infinite, but was instead one cube in an infinite ocean. The “infinite” ocean actually ended after a certain amount of blocks, depending on render distance. It was very popular to hack this version and obtain blocks that should not have been obtainable, or play early Survival in multiplayer by manipulating game files or using cheat engines.

ALPHA

June 30, 2010 – December 20, 2010

The fifth development phase of Minecraft, and the fourth to be playable by the public.
During this phase, very frequent and big updates were released, due to Notch taking development of the game as a full time job and starting a small company to support the game’s growth. The game also costed money to play now, and had to be downloaded.
New features included:

  • Survival multiplayer
  • The Nether – a completely new realm accessible only via creating a portal, which appeared as an enormous underground cave system made up of primarily lava and all new blocks and terrain. It also included mobs that do not appear in the overworld, such as Ghasts and the Zombie Pigmen. The realm is infinite in the x axis, but the y axis goes only up to a certain point, where a bedrock ceiling is located, with the Void being located above and below the Nether.
  • Biomes – new terrains with different trees, grass, temperatures, altitudes and chunk generation
  • Redstone circuits – a complex system of switches and logic that can make blocks move and interact
  • Blocks: netherrack, glowstone, soul sand, pumpkins, jack-o-lanterns
  • Mobs: cows, chickens, slime, ghasts, zombie pigmen
  • New sounds
  • Snowy and icy terrain
  • Boats
  • New music
  • More painting patterns
  • Increased world height
  • Heads Up Display
  • Screenshot taking
  • 40 inventory slots
  • Stairs



BETA

December 20, 2010 – November 18, 2011

Beta is the final development phase before the game was officially released in full. It is the fifth stage of the game. It had a few minor updates but included a price increase and a completely new logo and main menu screen.
Updates included:

  • Achievements and statistics
  • Server-side inventory
  • Full creative game mode
  • Weather
  • Variety in biomes and trees
  • Lighting mechanics
  • New graphical effects
  • Naturally generated structures – strongholds, villages, mineshafts
  • Mobs: squid, endermen, silverfish, cave spiders, wolves
  • Health mechanics and Hunger
  • Stackable food
  • Combat mechanics: experience, charged bows, sprinting, blocking
  • New redstone mechanisms
  • New farming elements
  • Maps
  • Beds
  • Dyes



JAVA EDITION

November 18, 2011– present

The full release of Minecraft. This phase has had 13 major updates until now, with the 14th one being in development right now.
Each large update consisted of multiple minor updates where new mechanics and items were added piece by piece, much like up until now.
